Here is the complete phase-wise roadmap for your AI Accounts Payable SaaS:

AI Accounts Payable SaaS — Full Roadmap
Phase 1 — Foundation & Core Experience
Goal: Get the core product working. A user can sign up, connect Gmail or upload an invoice, and see it processed on a clean dashboard.

What gets built:

Dedicated landing page for the AP product (its own entry point with its own button)
User onboarding flow — company setup, connecting Gmail, setting basic preferences
Invoice ingestion — Gmail connection pulls invoices automatically, plus manual PDF/image upload
AI invoice reader — reads the document and extracts vendor name, amount, due date, line items, invoice number
Invoice Inbox — a clean list of all received invoices with their status
Invoice Detail Page — full view of one invoice: original document + extracted data side by side
Core Dashboard — shows total invoices, pending review, flagged items, upcoming due dates
Basic data storage — every invoice saved securely in the database
What the user feels:
They connect Gmail, invoices start appearing automatically, AI reads them instantly. First impression is strong — "this is already saving me time."

Phase 2 — Intelligence & Validation
Goal: Add the AI brain. The system doesn't just read invoices — it checks, validates, and flags them.

What gets built:

Duplicate invoice detection — AI flags if the same invoice is submitted twice
Vendor verification — checks if the vendor is known or new/unrecognized
Amount anomaly detection — flags invoices that are unusually high compared to history
Smart flagging system — every issue explained in plain English, not accounting jargon
Vendor directory — a clean list of all vendors with their history, average invoice amount, payment track record
Invoice status tracking — New → Under Review → Approved → Paid, always visible
Search & filter — find any invoice by vendor, date, amount, or status instantly
Email notifications — user gets notified when a new invoice arrives or something needs attention
What the user feels:
The system catches problems they would have missed. They start trusting it. Confidence grows.

Phase 3 — Approval Workflow
Goal: Add team collaboration. Multiple people can be involved in approving invoices based on rules.

What gets built:

Team member invitations — add colleagues with defined roles (Admin, Approver, Viewer)
Approval rules engine — user sets rules like "invoices above $2,000 need CFO approval"
Approval request notifications — approvers get an email/in-app notification with one-click approve or reject
Approval history — every invoice shows a full audit trail of who approved what and when
Comments on invoices — team members can leave notes on any invoice
Bulk actions — approve or flag multiple invoices at once
Role-based access — each team member only sees what they're supposed to see
What the user feels:
No more WhatsApp back-and-forth for approvals. Everything is tracked, organized, and accountable.

Phase 4 — Payment Queue & Cash Intelligence
Goal: Help the user decide when and what to pay, without actually making the payment automatically.

What gets built:

Payment Queue — all approved invoices organized by urgency and due date
Early payment discount alerts — AI highlights invoices where paying early saves money
Cash flow forecast — simple view of how much is due this week, this month, next month
Payment status tracking — user marks invoices as paid, system records it
Accounting software sync — connects to QuickBooks and Xero, auto-posts every invoice and payment
Spend analytics — charts showing spending by vendor, by category, by month
Exportable reports — download PDF or Excel reports for accountants or auditors
What the user feels:
They never miss a due date. They see opportunities to save money. Their accountant is happy because everything is already organized.

Phase 5 — SaaS Infrastructure & Monetization
Goal: Turn this into a proper subscription business.

What gets built:

Subscription plans — Free tier (limited invoices/month), Pro, Business, Enterprise
Billing integration — Stripe-powered subscription management
Usage limits & upgrade prompts — when a free user hits their limit, smooth upgrade flow
Multi-company support — one account can manage multiple businesses
White-label option — agencies can offer this to their clients under their own brand
Admin panel — internal dashboard to manage users, subscriptions, and platform health
API access — larger businesses can connect their own systems to the platform
SOC 2 / data security groundwork — proper encryption, audit logs, compliance readiness
What the user feels:
It feels like a real product they're paying for and getting serious value from. Not a tool — a business asset.

Phase 6 — Scale & Advanced AI
Goal: Make the AI smarter and the product stickier.

What gets built:

Auto-payment execution — connects to bank APIs to pay approved invoices automatically (the feature we're skipping for now, added here when trust is established)
Vendor portal — vendors get their own link to submit invoices directly, no email needed
AI-suggested GL codes — automatically categorizes every invoice for accounting
Fraud detection model — trained specifically on invoice fraud patterns
Mobile app — native iOS and Android experience
Advanced analytics — benchmarking against industry averages, spend optimization suggestions
Integrations marketplace — plug into more accounting tools, ERPs, and banks
What the user feels:
The system is running in the background, handling everything. They barely need to touch it.
