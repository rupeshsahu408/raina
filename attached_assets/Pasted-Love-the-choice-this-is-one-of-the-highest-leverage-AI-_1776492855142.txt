AI HR & Recruitment Copilot — Product Workflow
The Core Problem It Solves
Hiring a single person takes an average of 3–6 weeks and involves 15+ manual steps. Small businesses and startups have no HR team, and even larger companies waste enormous time on repetitive screening, coordination, and paperwork. This product automates the entire funnel from job creation to offer letter.

Phase 1 — Job Setup (The Foundation)
Step 1 — Job Description Generator
The recruiter answers 5–6 simple questions:

Role title, team, seniority level
Key responsibilities in plain language
Must-have vs. nice-to-have skills
Salary range, location, work mode
The AI generates a fully formatted, bias-reduced job description, ready to post. No HR writing skills needed.

Step 2 — Scoring Rubric Creation
The AI automatically builds a scoring rubric from the JD — defining what a 5/5 candidate looks like vs. a 2/5, across skills, experience, and culture signals. The recruiter can edit it. This becomes the engine for all downstream screening.

Phase 2 — Candidate Pipeline (Ingestion & Screening)
Step 3 — Resume Ingestion
Candidates apply through a branded portal (your product's hosted page) or resumes are bulk-uploaded. The AI parses every resume — structured extraction of skills, years of experience, previous companies, education, and gaps.

Step 4 — Automated Scoring
Every candidate is scored against the rubric from Step 2. The dashboard shows a ranked list — not just a score, but a breakdown: "Strong on Python, weak on leadership experience, missing cloud infrastructure background." The recruiter doesn't read 200 resumes — they see 20 that matter.

Step 5 — Red Flag Detection
The AI flags patterns: unexplained employment gaps, inflated titles, job-hopping without progression, or skills that appear in the resume but are suspiciously absent from any actual project context.

Phase 3 — First-Round Assessment (Async AI Interview)
Step 6 — Async Text or Video Interview
Shortlisted candidates receive an invitation to complete an async assessment — either:

Text-based (answer 5 structured questions in writing), or
Video-based (record short answers to AI-generated questions tailored to the role)
Questions are role-specific and generated by the AI from the rubric.

Step 7 — AI Analysis of Responses
The AI evaluates written or transcribed video answers for: clarity of thinking, relevance of examples, communication quality, red flags in claims, and alignment with rubric criteria. Each candidate gets an updated composite score.

Phase 4 — Recruiter Workflow (The Dashboard)
Step 8 — The Recruitment Dashboard
The central command center:

Kanban pipeline view: Applied → Screened → Assessed → Interview → Offer → Hired/Rejected
Candidate profile cards with AI summary, scores, red flags, and full resume
One-click to advance, reject with template email, or request more info
Collaboration: leave notes, tag teammates, assign decisions
Step 9 — AI Interview Preparation Brief
Before a human interview, the recruiter gets an AI-generated brief: "Ask about the gap between 2021–2022. Probe their claim of leading a team of 12. They scored low on system design — test this."

Phase 5 — Offer & Closure
Step 10 — Offer Letter Generator
Once a candidate is selected, the recruiter fills in compensation and start date. The AI generates a professional offer letter, customized to the role, ready to send or export to PDF.

Step 11 — Rejection Emails
At every rejection stage, the AI drafts a personalized, respectful rejection email — not a generic one. Candidates remember the experience.

Phase 6 — Intelligence Layer (What Makes It SaaS-Level)
Analytics Dashboard:

Average time-to-hire per role
Drop-off rates at each stage
Source quality (which job board sends the best candidates)
Bias detection reports (gender/age distribution across stages)
AI Memory:

"We rejected this candidate 8 months ago for X reason" — surfaces automatically when someone reapplies
Build a talent pool of silver-medal candidates for future roles
